Department of Cognitive Science
University of California, San Diego

Head of Outreach, Graduate Women in Computing

My research is in the field of human-computer interaction, information retrieval and cognitive science. I take an interdisciplinary approach to my research, using methods and theories from psychology, computer science, and learning science to design novel computational techniques to improve web search and collaboration. My research aims to help people engage in exploratory thinking, with applications in creativity support tools and pedagogy.

Prior to starting my PhD, I graduated summa cum laude from Mount Holyoke College with a bachelors degree, double majoring in Computer Science and Psychology (specializing in Cognitive Neuroscience).

Outside of research, I am passionate about establishing mentorship programs to bridge the gender gap in technology, and teaching design and computational thinking courses. In my free time, I love playing squash, and exploring new beaches and hiking trails.
